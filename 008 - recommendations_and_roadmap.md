# Recommendations & Roadmap

### Overview


A cyber security consultant's recommendation section should be **concise, evidence-based, and focused on management decisions**. It should not repeat the controls already documented in the Risk Treatment Plan. Instead, it should explain **why the recommendations matter, what strategic direction Urban Thread should take, and how security supports business growth**.

Below is a revised version reduced by approximately 50% and written in a professional consultancy style.


# 8. Recommendations & Roadmap

## 8.1 Overview

The risk assessment identified that Urban Thread's primary cyber security challenges are driven by its dependence on cloud services, online sales channels, third-party providers, customer data processing, and limited internal security resources.

The organisation has adopted an appropriate technology model for a growing retail business by using SaaS platforms such as e-commerce, payment processing and cloud productivity services. However, this approach requires strong identity protection, secure configuration, supplier oversight and effective governance.

The recommended approach is not to implement expensive enterprise security solutions, but to establish a proportionate security capability that reduces the most significant risks while supporting business growth.

The priority areas identified are:

1. Protecting customer and business data.
2. Reducing identity and account compromise risks.
3. Improving resilience against cyber incidents.
4. Strengthening third-party risk management.
5. Establishing security governance suitable for future expansion.



# 8.2 Strategic Recommendations

## Recommendation 1 – Establish Strong Identity Security Controls

The assessment identified compromised user accounts as a key risk because Urban Thread relies heavily on cloud-based platforms, including Microsoft 365, Shopify, payment services and social media channels.

Identity security should therefore be treated as a core business control rather than an IT function. The organisation should continue implementing the identity protection measures identified within the Risk Treatment Plan to reduce exposure to phishing, credential theft and unauthorised access.

**Expected business outcome:**

* Reduced likelihood of account compromise.
* Improved protection of customer and financial information.
* Increased confidence in cloud service usage.



## Recommendation 2 – Strengthen Customer Data Protection and Compliance

Urban Thread's handling of customer information creates significant regulatory responsibility, particularly because a substantial proportion of customers are under 18. A breach involving customer data could result in regulatory action, financial penalties and reputational damage.

The organisation should continue developing its privacy governance approach by ensuring that customer data is collected, processed, stored and shared appropriately.

**Expected business outcome:**

* Reduced GDPR compliance risk.
* Improved customer trust.
* Better control over personal information throughout its lifecycle.



## Recommendation 3 – Improve Cyber Resilience

As Urban Thread's revenue increasingly depends on online sales and digital services, cyber incidents could directly affect business operations. The ability to respond effectively and restore services quickly is therefore essential.

The organisation should mature its incident response and recovery capability as outlined in the Risk Treatment Plan, ensuring that cyber security incidents are managed as business continuity events rather than purely technical issues.

**Expected business outcome:**

* Reduced operational downtime.
* Faster recovery following cyber incidents.
* Improved decision-making during security events.



## Recommendation 4 – Introduce Formal Security Governance

Urban Thread's current approach is appropriate for a small organisation, but planned expansion into additional stores will increase the complexity of managing users, devices, suppliers and customer information.

Cyber security responsibilities should become more structured as the business grows, with clear ownership, regular reviews and documented processes.

**Expected business outcome:**

* Improved accountability.
* Better alignment between security investment and business priorities.
* A sustainable security approach that supports expansion.



## Recommendation 5 – Manage Third-Party Cyber Risk

Urban Thread's reliance on external providers reduces operational overhead but introduces dependency risks. The organisation remains responsible for ensuring that suppliers handling customer information maintain appropriate security practices.

Supplier assurance should become part of normal business management rather than a one-time activity.

**Expected business outcome:**

* Reduced supply chain exposure.
* Improved regulatory assurance.
* Greater confidence in third-party services.



# 8.3 Security Roadmap

The roadmap below provides a strategic view of how Urban Thread's cyber security capability should mature alongside business growth.

| Business Stage                   | Timeline         | Strategic Objective                                                                                                                                |
| -------------------------------- | ---------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Security Foundation**          | 0–3 months       | Establish essential security controls and reduce immediate exposure from account compromise, phishing and data breaches.                           |
| **Security Improvement**         | 3–6 months       | Strengthen governance, privacy management, supplier assurance and operational resilience.                                                          |
| **Business Expansion Readiness** | 6–12 months      | Develop scalable security processes to support additional stores, increased workforce and greater technology dependence.                           |
| **Future Security Maturity**     | Beyond 12 months | Consider additional security investment, outsourced security expertise and alignment with recognised frameworks as business requirements increase. |



# 8.4 Measuring Progress

Urban Thread should measure improvement through practical security indicators rather than technology ownership alone.

Recommended measures include:

| Area                | Success Measure                                               |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Identity Security   | All critical business accounts protected by MFA               |
| Data Protection     | GDPR reviews and privacy assessments completed periodically   |
| Staff Awareness     | Employees receive regular cyber security training             |
| Resilience          | Backups and recovery procedures tested successfully           |
| Governance          | Risk register reviewed at least annually                      |
| Supplier Management | Key vendors reviewed for security and compliance requirements |



# 8.5 Conclusion

Urban Thread does not require an enterprise-level cyber security programme at its current stage of development. However, its reliance on digital platforms, customer data and online sales means that basic security controls must be implemented consistently and managed effectively.

The recommended roadmap provides a proportionate approach that balances security requirements with business realities. By focusing first on identity protection, data governance, resilience and supplier management, Urban Thread can reduce its most significant cyber risks while creating a secure foundation for future growth.

As the organisation expands, cyber security should evolve from an operational responsibility into a formal business capability that supports customer trust, regulatory compliance and long-term resilience.
